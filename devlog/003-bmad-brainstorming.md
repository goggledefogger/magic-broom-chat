# 003 — BMAD Brainstorming Session

**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Framework:** bmad
**Phase:** Brainstorming (pre-PRD ideation)

## What Was Completed

Ran the `bmad-brainstorming` skill to explore feature ideas, prioritization, course tailoring, and project personality for Magic Brooms. Used AI-Recommended technique approach with three techniques: Role Playing, SCAMPER Method, and What If Scenarios.

## Key Decisions

- **Sorcerer's Apprentice** chosen as the unifying metaphor — emerged organically from a prior conversation Danny referenced, not planned
- **Design philosophy:** "useful first, clever second" and "meta only when it teaches"
- **Central design tension:** "leveling the playing field without flattening it" — serving students at every skill level
- **No topic threading for MVP** — intentionally skipping Zulip's signature feature to ship simpler
- **Pull-based notifications only** — unread badges yes, push interruptions no

## 22 Ideas, 5 Themes

1. **Student Empowerment & Contribution Architecture** — tiered contributions, modular architecture, tool-agnostic workflow, visible attribution
2. **Showcase & Portfolio Ecosystem** — showcase channel type, collaborative brainstorming, portfolio-as-byproduct
3. **AI-Native Platform** — LLM-ready data export/MCP, broom companion bot, built-in image generation, activity-derived presence
4. **Identity & Delight** — Sorcerer's Apprentice theming, generative SVG avatars, Easter eggs, playful error messages
5. **Thoughtful UX Constraints** — no topic threading, pull notifications, GitHub integration, contextual help threads

## Process Observations

- ~15 minutes of back-and-forth across three techniques (per original session log)
- The BMAD brainstorming skill provided structure: technique selection, phased execution, organization
- Skipped the end-user persona in Role Playing and the Modify lens in SCAMPER — facilitator was responsive to "let's move on"
- Organization step synthesized themes and priorities from the free-form ideation
- The strongest ideas came from Role Playing (student + instructor perspectives), not from the more structured SCAMPER
- The Sorcerer's Apprentice metaphor emerged during What If Scenarios
