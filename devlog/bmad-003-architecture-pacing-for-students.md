# BMAD-003 — Architecture Process: Pacing Guidance for Students

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Framework:** bmad
**Phase:** Architecture design (teaching observation)

## Observation

BMAD's architecture workflow has many steps — enough that it could feel overwhelming for students encountering it for the first time. But the content at each step is actually good. The structured questions force thinking about things (tech stack tradeoffs, data model decisions, integration patterns) that would otherwise get skipped.

## Recommended Framing for the Course

Two paths depending on the student's comfort level:

1. **"Just go for it"** — Keep saying continue, don't stress about understanding every step, get to the end and see the full output. Especially early on in learning about AI-assisted development, reaching completion matters more than understanding every intermediate step. Feeling like the process is too long and quitting is the worst outcome.

2. **"Slow down and explore"** — For students who want to go deeper: pause at each step, ask the framework to explain what it's doing and why, understand the reasoning behind the questions. This is where the real learning happens — but it only works if the student already believes the process leads somewhere valuable.

## Teaching Takeaway

Completion before comprehension. Let students see the end result first, then circle back to understand the steps. The value of BMAD's structured approach becomes obvious in hindsight (especially compared to vanilla's "just build it" approach), but it's hard to appreciate mid-process when you're on step 7 of 12.
